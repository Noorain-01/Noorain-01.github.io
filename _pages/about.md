I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at the School of Computing, Macquarie University, Sydney, Australia, supervised by Distinguished Professor Michael Sheng and Associate Professor Adnan Mahmood. My research lies at the intersection of Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence, Federated Learning, and Distributed Machine Learning, with a particular focus on developing learning algorithms that are fair, robust, adaptive, and reliable in decentralized environments. My current research investigates how heterogeneous and evolving data distributions influence representation learning and model performance in federated learning. I develop principled algorithms for fairness-aware representation learning, client selection, adaptive aggregation, and bias mitigation under dynamic environments.

My work has appeared in leading AI and data mining venues, including AAAI 2026, IJCAI 2025, The Web Conference (WWW) 2025, and the Advanced Intelligent Systems journal. I currently have ongoing work on representation learning, temporal distribution drift, and trustworthy federated learning for top-tier conferences and journals. Alongside my research, I enjoy teaching and mentoring students. I currently teach Artificial Intelligence, Cloud and Edge Computing, Embedded Systems, and Programming at Macquarie University and Central Queensland University, where I strive to bridge theoretical foundations with practical machine learning applications.

My research has been recognized through the International Macquarie Research Excellence Scholarship, the Research Rising Star Award, the Macquarie University Postgraduate Research Fund, an IJCAI Travel Grant, and an IEEE EPICS Research Grant. I also actively contribute to the research community by serving as a Technical Session Chair and Program Committee member for several leading international AI conferences.

I am always interested in collaborating on research related to trustworthy machine learning, federated learning, representation learning, and distributed AI systems.
